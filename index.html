The Formula
Composite Score Formula (out of 100)
Composite = (JIPMAT ÷ 400 × 70) + (10th% ÷ 100 × 15) + (12th% ÷ 100 × 15)
Safe JIPMAT = [(targetComposite − (10th% × 0.15) − (12th% × 0.15)) ÷ 70] × 400
targetComposite = Each category's 2024 cutoff converted to the new 100-point composite system, then +5% safety buffer applied.
Every category uses its own benchmark — General uses 304, EWS uses 247, and so on.

The Most Important Insight for 2025
Every 10% higher in boards saves ~8 marks on JIPMAT — this is now the single biggest lever a student can pull.

For General category: A 60/60 student needs ~349/400. A 90/90 student needs only ~297/400 — that's 52 marks of cushion from academics alone.

For SC/ST/PwD: The base cutoffs are lower, so academic marks matter even more as a percentage of your total composite. A 90/90 ST student may need as little as ~130/400 on JIPMAT — a highly achievable target.
